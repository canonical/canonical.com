Popular etcd use cases

{{ image(url="https://assets.ubuntu.com/v1/df6340f7-kubernetes.png", alt="", width="568", height="852", hi_def=True, loading="lazy", attrs={"class": "p-image-container__image"}) | safe }}

Kubernetes control plane storage

etcd is the de-facto data store for Kubernetes. Using Canonical Kubernetes together with our etcd artifacts is the best way to get reliable and securely maintained K8s deployments.

{{ image(url="https://assets.ubuntu.com/v1/a6204781-configuration_management.png", alt="", width="568", height="852", hi_def=True, loading="lazy", attrs={"class": "p-image-container__image"}) | safe }}

Configuration management

etcd is a highly reliable and strongly consistent key-value store, which makes it a foundational component for building resilient and fault-tolerant systems. For example, it can be used as the source of truth for configuration management among diverse applications.

{{ image(url="https://assets.ubuntu.com/v1/7aac50b3-service_discovery.png", alt="", width="568", height="852", hi_def=True, loading="lazy", attrs={"class": "p-image-container__image"}) | safe }}

Service discovery

Due to its strong consistency and simple API, etcd is often used for service discovery in geo-distributed setups. Typically, service providers will publish their endpoints in etcd and consumers will regularly check it for any endpoint change.

etcd is a good alternative to Apache Zookeeper or Consul for service discovery related use cases.

{{ image(url="https://assets.ubuntu.com/v1/ca9ba3c8-distributed_locking.png", alt="", width="568", height="852", hi_def=True, loading="lazy", attrs={"class": "p-image-container__image"}) | safe }}

Distributed locking

When operations must be coordinated across multiple nodes, you can either rely on a central orchestrator to serialize them or implement distributed locking to ensure mutual exclusion. Thanks to its strong serializability guarantees, etcd is a reliable foundation for implementing these patterns.
